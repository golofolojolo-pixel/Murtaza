API announces 47th edition of foundational Line Pipe standard - World Pipelines

The American Petroleum Institute has published the 47th edition of API Specification 5L for line pipe. This foundational standard outlines requirements for the safe manufacture of seamless and welded steel pipe used to transport oil, gas, and other energy products. The latest edition introduces critical requirements across more than 15 topic areas. Key updates focus on high-frequency weld pipe quality and requirements for pipelines transporting carbon dioxide. Technical revisions address hydrotest pressure gauge calibration, non-destructive evaluation personnel certification, and magnetic particle testing. The standard also updates guidelines for tensile test flattening, record retention, and sour-service performance. Additional changes include chemical element ranges, impact toughness requirements, lamination control, and sulfide stress cracking test requirements. Manufacturers capable of meeting these updated criteria can apply for the voluntary API Monogram licensing program. The new standard incorporates technical feedback and insights from subject matter experts representing more than a dozen countries.
